ScanForProfit gives you two ways to get your data out of the app. The **CSV export** generates a file you upload directly to eBay Seller Hub to create draft listings in bulk. The **JSON backup** saves everything — inventory, expenses, scan history, and settings — to a single file you can store anywhere and restore from at any time.

---

## CSV Export: Send Draft Listings to eBay

The CSV export creates an eBay Seller Hub–compatible bulk listing file from your ready-to-list inventory. Instead of entering each listing manually in eBay, you upload one file and all your drafts appear at once.

### Eligibility

Only items with a status of **Unlisted** and an AI-generated listing are included in the export. If an item doesn't have a listing yet, generate one from the Listing Generator tab first.

### Export Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the CSV Export screen">
    From the **Listing** tab, tap **Export to eBay CSV**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your time window">
    Select the window of items to include: **Last 24 hours**, **Last 48 hours**, or **Last 7 days**. This filters by when you added the item to inventory, not when you generated the listing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the preview">
    The app shows you a preview of every item that will be included. Confirm the titles, prices, and SKUs look correct.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the CSV">
    Tap **Download CSV**. On mobile, the share sheet opens and you can save the file to Files, Google Drive, or email it to yourself. On the web app, the file downloads directly to your device.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### What's in the CSV

The file uses eBay's Seller Hub bulk draft format and includes the following columns for each item:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Action** | Always `Add` — creates a new draft listing |
| **Custom label (SKU)** | Your ScanForProfit SKU (e.g. `ELEC-00042`) |
| **Category ID** | eBay leaf category ID mapped from your item category |
| **Title** | AI-generated listing title (max 80 characters) |
| **Price** | Your sell price as entered in inventory |
| **Quantity** | Always `1` |
| **Condition ID** | eBay condition code mapped from your item condition |
| **Description** | AI-generated listing description |

### Uploading to eBay Seller Hub

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log into eBay Seller Hub">
    Go to [ebay.com/sh/lst](https://www.ebay.com/sh/lst) and sign in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Listings → Import">
    From the **Listings** menu, select **Import listings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload your CSV file">
    Click **Upload file**, select the CSV you downloaded from ScanForProfit, and click **Submit**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and activate">
    eBay imports the file as draft listings. Review each draft, make any final edits, and click **List item** on each one — or select all and use the bulk activate option.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## JSON Backup: Save Everything

The JSON backup captures a complete snapshot of your ScanForProfit account: all inventory items, expenses, scan history, and your current settings.

### Download a Backup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Backup screen">
    From the **Stats** tab, tap **Subscription & Account**, then tap **Download Backup**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the file">
    The app generates a `.json` file named with today's date (e.g., `scanforprofit-backup-2026-06-01.json`). Save it to iCloud, Google Drive, email it to yourself, or store it anywhere you keep important files.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The backup includes **photo metadata** (item names, SKUs, categories, notes) but not the photo files themselves. Photos remain stored on your device. If you restore on a new device, item data will be restored but photos will not carry over.
</Note>

### Restore from Backup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Import screen">
    From the **Stats** tab, tap **Subscription & Account**, then tap **Import / Restore**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your backup file">
    Tap **Choose file** and navigate to your `.json` backup. Select it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the preview">
    The app shows you how many items, expenses, and scan entries it found in the file. Confirm the numbers look right.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the restore">
    Tap **Import**. Existing data is merged with the backup — duplicate SKUs are skipped to avoid overwriting items you've updated since the backup was made.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## CSV Import: Migrate from a Spreadsheet

If you've been tracking inventory in a spreadsheet, you can import it into ScanForProfit as a CSV file without re-entering every item manually.

Your CSV needs a `nickname` column at minimum. The following column names are recognized automatically:

| Column name | Maps to |
|---|---|
| `nickname` | Item name (required) |
| `cost` | Purchase price |
| `sellPrice` | Target sell price |
| `category` | Item category |
| `condition` | Item condition |
| `status` | Inventory status (Unlisted, Listed, Sold) |

To import: go to **Stats → Import / Restore**, tap **Import CSV**, select your file, review the preview, and confirm.

<Tip>
  Back up your data before you do a major inventory cleanup or before clearing sold items. It takes ten seconds and means you always have a recoverable copy of your full history.
</Tip>
