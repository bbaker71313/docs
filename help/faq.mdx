## Getting Started

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to download an app?">
    No download required. ScanForProfit is a web app — open your browser, go to [scanforprofit.com/app.html](https://scanforprofit.com/app.html), and you're in. It works on your phone's browser just like a native app, so you can scan items at the thrift store without ever visiting an app store.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is ScanForProfit free?">
    Yes. The **Scout** plan is free forever — no trial period, no expiration. You get 25 AI scans per month, up to 10 inventory items, the full listing generator, and eBay export. Paid plans unlock higher limits and additional features when you're ready to scale.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need a credit card to sign up?">
    No. Creating a free Scout account requires only your email address and a password. You won't be asked for payment information unless you choose to upgrade to a paid plan.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I sign up?">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the app">
        Go to [scanforprofit.com/app.html](https://scanforprofit.com/app.html) in your browser.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Create your account">
        Tap **Create Account**, enter your email address and a password, then submit the form.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify your email">
        Check your inbox for a verification email and click the confirmation link. You won't be able to log in until your email is verified.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Start scanning">
        Log in and take your first scan. That's it.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I didn't receive my verification email — what do I do?">
    First, check your spam or junk folder — verification emails sometimes land there. If it's not there, wait a few minutes and check again. Email delivery can occasionally take a couple of minutes.

    If you still haven't received it after 10 minutes, contact [support@scanforprofit.com](mailto:support@scanforprofit.com) with your account email address and we'll sort it out for you.

    <Note>
      Make sure you're checking the inbox for the exact email address you used when signing up.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Scanning

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How many scans do I get per month?">
    Your monthly scan count depends on your plan:

    | Plan | Scans/Month |
    |------|-------------|
    | Scout (free) | 25 |
    | Hustle | Unlimited |
    | Stack | Unlimited |
    | Empire | Unlimited |

    Your current usage is always visible in the tier banner at the top of the app.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="When does my scan count reset?">
    Your scan count resets on the same calendar date each month — the date you created your account. After it resets, your full monthly allowance is restored automatically. You don't need to do anything.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does a shelf scan count toward my monthly limit?">
    Yes. Each shelf scan uses 1 scan from your monthly allowance — the same as a single-item scan. A shelf scan evaluates every item visible in the photo, so one scan can surface a dozen profitable items at once.

    <Tip>
      Use shelf scans to stretch your monthly limit further. One wide photo of a shelf covers more ground than scanning each item one by one.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How accurate are the AI scan results?">
    The AI gives you a fast, informed starting point based on eBay market knowledge — typically accurate enough to make a confident sourcing decision in seconds. That said, results can vary based on item condition, regional demand, and how well the photo captures the item.

    <Warning>
      Always verify high-value items manually before buying. Use the AI result as your first filter, not your final word — especially on items over $50.
    </Warning>

    The HOT/LIST/SKIP decision is also shaped by your personal settings (your target ROI, eBay fee rate, and cost threshold). If your settings don't reflect how you actually operate, the decisions won't match your expectations. See the question below about HOT/SKIP decisions.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I scan an item without taking a photo?">
    Yes. You can type an item name or description directly into the scan field instead of uploading a photo. This is useful when an item is hard to photograph or when you already know what you're looking at and just want the profit math.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why did I get a SKIP on something I know sells well?">
    The HOT/LIST/SKIP decision is based entirely on your configured settings — your target ROI, your eBay fee rate, your minimum profit threshold, and your sourcing cost. If those settings don't match how you actually flip items, the AI's decision won't match your expectations either.

    Open **Settings** and review your fee and profit targets. Adjusting them to reflect your real numbers will make the HOT/LIST/SKIP decisions more useful. If you're unsure what to set, start with the defaults and tune from there.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Inventory & Listings

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I use ScanForProfit on multiple devices?">
    Yes. Your account — including inventory metadata, scan history, and listing data — syncs across all devices where you're logged in. You can be signed in on your phone, a tablet, and a computer at the same time.

    The one exception is photos. See the next question for details.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to my photos if I switch devices?">
    Photos are stored in your browser on the device where you took them. They don't sync to the cloud or transfer to other devices.

    If you switch phones or clear your browser data, photos attached to inventory items will no longer be visible — but the item's text data (cost, title, price, status) will still be there on all your devices.

    <Tip>
      If you need photos to be accessible across devices, consider saving them to your phone's photo library as a backup before clearing browser data.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I generate an eBay listing?">
    Open any item in your **Inventory** tab and tap the listing generator. The AI will draft a complete eBay listing — title, description, category suggestion, and condition notes — based on the item details and scan results. The listing generator is available on all plans, including the free Scout plan.

    <Tip>
      The more detail you add to your item (brand, model, condition, any flaws), the better the generated listing will be.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I export listings to eBay?">
    From the **Listing** tab, you can export your listings as a CSV file formatted for eBay Seller Hub's bulk upload tool. You can also connect your eBay account via OAuth in **Settings** to enable direct listing sync (available on paid plans).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What does the AI listing generator need to work well?">
    The generator works best when you give it clear item details: the brand, model or product name, condition, any notable features, and any flaws. A descriptive item title and a sharp photo help the AI produce a specific, accurate listing rather than a generic one.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Plans & Billing

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What happens when I hit my inventory item limit?">
    When you reach your plan's inventory limit, you won't be able to add new items until you either upgrade your plan or archive items you've already sold. Archiving removes items from your active inventory count while keeping their data in your records.

    The Scout plan includes 10 inventory items. Upgrade to Hustle for 500 items, or Stack/Empire for unlimited.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I cancel anytime?">
    Yes. You can cancel your paid plan at any time. Your access to paid features continues until the end of your current billing period, after which your account reverts to the free Scout tier.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to my data if I cancel?">
    Your inventory, scan history, and listing data stay in your account when you downgrade to Scout. You won't lose your records. You will lose access to paid-only features (like advanced P&L, unlimited scans, and eBay listing sync) until you resubscribe.

    <Tip>
      Export a JSON backup of your inventory before canceling, so you have a copy outside the app.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is there a discount for annual billing?">
    Yes. All paid plans offer a discount when you pay annually:

    | Plan | Monthly | Annual (total/yr) |
    |------|---------|-------------------|
    | Hustle | $19/mo | $180/yr |
    | Stack | $49/mo | $468/yr |
    | Empire | $199/mo | $1,908/yr |
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
