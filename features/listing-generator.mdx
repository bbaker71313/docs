Writing a good eBay listing takes time you don't have when you've got 30 items sitting in a pile. The Listing Generator uses AI to write a complete, keyword-optimized eBay listing for any inventory item in seconds — including a search-ready title, a full description with bullet points, and a condition note. What used to take 20 minutes per item now takes under a minute, and the output is already formatted for eBay Seller Hub bulk upload.

---

## How to Generate a Listing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the item">
    In the **Inventory** tab, tap the item you want to list to open its detail view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Generate Listing">
    Tap the **Generate Listing** button. The AI reads the item's name, category, condition, and any notes you've added.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the generated listing">
    The generator returns a title, full description, and condition note. Read through each section before saving.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Regenerate if needed">
    Not happy with the result? Tap **Regenerate** to get a new version. You can regenerate as many times as you like.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the listing">
    Tap **Save**. The listing attaches to the inventory item and the item becomes eligible for CSV export to eBay Seller Hub.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Gets Generated

Each generated listing contains three parts:

### Title

A keyword-optimized eBay title up to **80 characters** — the maximum eBay allows. The AI puts the most important search terms first, since eBay's search algorithm weights the beginning of the title heavily.

**Example (Electronics):**
> `Sony WH-1000XM4 Wireless Noise Canceling Headphones Black - Tested Works`

**Example (Clothing):**
> `Levi's 501 Original Fit Jeans Men's 34x32 Dark Wash Straight Leg Button Fly`

The title avoids filler words like "look," "rare," "wow," or "L@@K" — eBay's algorithm ignores them and buyers skip past them.

### Description

A **250–400 word** description written in a mobile-friendly format with bullet points for key details. The AI tailors the focus to the item's category:

- **Electronics** — highlights functional status, tested results, and specifications
- **Clothing** — covers fabric, fit, brand, styling notes, and measurements
- **Shoes** — leads with size, brand, condition, and fit notes
- **Home & Garden** — covers materials, dimensions, and functionality
- **Collectibles** — emphasizes authenticity, rarity, and condition details
- **Toys & Hobbies** — covers completeness, vintage value, and condition
- **Sporting Goods** — leads with brand, specifications, and condition
- **Books** — notes author, edition, binding type, and condition
- **Jewelry & Watches** — covers material, brand, and specifications

### Condition Note

A **50–100 word** condition note that's specific about what you can see — scratches, wear, missing parts, original packaging. This populates the eBay condition description field and reduces buyer questions and return requests.

**Example:**
> *Screen is bright and responsive with no cracks or scratches. Body shows light scuffs on the back cover consistent with normal use. All buttons and ports tested and working. Original charger included. Battery holds charge well.*

## Regenerating a Listing

If the first result doesn't fit your style or misses something important, tap **Regenerate**. Each regeneration produces a fresh version — different word choices, different emphasis, same item details. Most sellers find the second or third result hits the mark.

<Tip>
If you keep getting generic descriptions, go back and add more detail to the item's **Notes** field before regenerating. The AI works with what you give it — the more specific your notes, the more specific the listing.
</Tip>

## Saving the Listing

Tap **Save** to store the generated listing to the inventory item. Once saved:

- The full title, description, and condition note are attached to the item record
- The item becomes eligible for CSV export to eBay Seller Hub

You can edit any saved listing by going back to the item's detail view, tapping **Edit**, and re-running the generator or editing the fields directly.

## Exporting to eBay Seller Hub

Once you've saved listings for one or more items, you can export them all as a single CSV file that uploads directly to eBay Seller Hub's **Import Listings** tool. This is the fastest way to go from a pile of sourced items to active eBay listings.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Save listings for the items you want to export">
    Make sure every item you want to post has a saved listing. Items without a saved listing won't appear in the export.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the CSV Export screen">
    Tap the **Export** option in the Inventory tab menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a time window">
    Choose how far back to look: last **24 hours**, **48 hours**, or **past week**. The export includes all Unlisted items with a saved listing that haven't been exported before, within that window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the CSV">
    Tap **Download CSV**. The file is saved to your device and ready to upload.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload to eBay Seller Hub">
    Open **eBay Seller Hub** in a browser, go to **Listings → Import Listings**, and upload the CSV file. eBay creates draft listings for every row — review them and publish when ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The CSV format matches eBay's official Seller Hub bulk draft listings template. Each row includes all fields Seller Hub needs to create a complete draft:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Action** | Draft listing action |
| **Custom label (SKU)** | Your item's auto-generated SKU |
| **Category ID** | eBay category ID matched to your item's category |
| **Title** | The AI-generated, keyword-optimized title |
| **UPC** | Left blank (fill in Seller Hub if known) |
| **Price** | Your target selling price |
| **Quantity** | 1 |
| **Item photo URL** | Photo reference if available |
| **Condition ID** | eBay condition code mapped from your item's condition |
| **Description** | The full AI-generated description |

<Note>
eBay may flag some drafts for category or condition review before you can publish them. This is normal — just confirm the suggested values in Seller Hub and publish.
</Note>

## Tips for Better Listings

The Listing Generator is only as good as the item data you feed it. These habits consistently produce stronger output:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Write specific notes" icon="pencil">
    Add brand, model number, size, color, and any flaws to the **Notes** field before generating. "Nike running shoes" produces a weaker listing than "Nike Air Zoom Pegasus 40 Men's Size 11 Gray/White — minor sole wear."
  </Card>
  <Card title="Set the right condition" icon="star">
    Pick the most accurate condition before generating. The condition note the AI writes is based on what you select — "Good" and "Fair" produce very different outputs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Include what's in the box" icon="box-open">
    If you have original packaging, charger, manual, or accessories, note it. These details improve both the description and the condition note, and buyers search for them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use clear photos" icon="camera">
    Better item photos in your inventory often help the AI detection step produce better notes — which leads to better listings. Run the Photo Agent on dark or blurry shots before generating.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
