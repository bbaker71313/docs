Profit Hub is your financial command center. It pulls data directly from your sold inventory and manually entered expenses to show you exactly what your reselling business made — by week, month, or year. No spreadsheets, no guessing. Open the Stats tab and see your real numbers.

## Overview Tab: Your Business at a Glance

The Overview tab greets you with four KPI cards at the top of the dashboard.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Total Sales" icon="dollar-sign">
    The combined actual sale prices of every item you've marked as Sold within your selected time period.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Net Profit" icon="chart-line">
    Total sales minus your cost of goods, eBay fees, packaging, and any expenses you've logged. This is what you actually kept.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Items Sold" icon="tag">
    The count of inventory items marked Sold in the selected period — a quick read on your sales velocity.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Active Listings" icon="list">
    How many items in your inventory currently have a Listed status — your live selling floor at a glance.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Filtering by Time Period

Tap the filter controls at the top of the dashboard to switch between **This Week**, **This Month**, and **This Year**. Every KPI card and chart on the page updates instantly to reflect the selected window, so you can compare your weekly pace against your monthly trend without leaving the screen.

### Sourcing Activity and Inventory Summary

Below the KPI cards, the Overview tab also shows today's scan count, a summary of your inventory by status, and a shortcut into Profit Compass for growth insights. Use these cards as quick navigation to whatever part of your business needs attention.

## P&L Tab: Full Profit and Loss Tracking

Switch to the **P&L** sub-tab for your complete profit-and-loss statement. Everything here is calculated from two sources: the sold items in your inventory and the expenses you add manually.

### How Revenue Is Tracked

Revenue populates automatically from your inventory. Every item you mark as Sold with an actual sale price becomes a line in your P&L. The app uses that real sale price — not your original estimated price — along with your configured eBay fee rate and packaging cost to calculate the net profit on each sale.

<Tip>
  Always enter the actual sale price when you mark an item as Sold. That number flows directly into your P&L. If you leave it blank or use a placeholder, your revenue and profit figures will be off.
</Tip>

### Adding Expenses

Log any business cost directly in the P&L tab. Tap **Add Expense** and fill in:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a type">
    Choose a category for the expense — supplies, shipping materials, storage, tools, or any other business cost.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the amount">
    Type the dollar amount for this expense.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the date">
    Set the date the expense occurred so it lands in the right reporting period.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a description">
    Optionally add a note — for example, "bubble wrap from Amazon" or "storage unit April" — so you remember what the line item was at tax time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

All expenses reduce your net profit and appear as individual line items in the P&L report.

### Mileage Log

Sourcing trips are a deductible business expense. Log your miles in the Mileage section and the app automatically calculates your IRS deduction.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter miles driven">
    Type in the number of miles for a sourcing trip, supply run, or any other business-related drive.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the deduction">
    The app multiplies your miles by the current IRS standard mileage rate ($0.67/mile) and adds the result to your expenses automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The IRS standard mileage rate is reviewed annually. ScanForProfit updates the rate to match the current IRS guidelines each year — check the rate shown in the Mileage section if you need to confirm the figure being used.
</Note>

### Tax Reserve Estimate

Profit Hub sets aside a **25% tax reserve** based on your net profit and displays it as an estimate in the P&L summary. This is a planning figure — not tax advice — but it gives you a working number to keep in a separate account so a quarterly tax bill doesn't catch you off guard.

<Warning>
  The 25% tax reserve is an estimate. Your actual tax liability depends on your total income, deductions, business structure, and jurisdiction. Consult a tax professional for guidance specific to your situation.
</Warning>

### Monthly Breakdown

Scroll down in the P&L tab to see a month-by-month table of sales and profit. Use this to spot which months you source most effectively, identify seasonal patterns in your categories, and set realistic goals for upcoming months.

## Charts: Visualizing Your Performance

The Stats tab includes three charts to help you see patterns that raw numbers can hide.

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="Profit by Category" icon="chart-bar">
    A bar chart showing net profit broken out by inventory category — Electronics, Clothing, Collectibles, and so on. Use it to see at a glance which categories are carrying your business and which ones are dragging on your margins.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Trend Line" icon="chart-line">
    A rolling six-period trend line showing profit over your last six weeks, months, or years depending on your active filter. An upward slope means your business is growing; a flat or declining line tells you something needs to change.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Best and Worst Performers" icon="arrow-up-arrow-down">
    A ranked view of your top-earning and lowest-earning inventory items. Best performers confirm which types of items to keep sourcing. Worst performers are candidates for a price drop, a relist, or a donation run.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Tips for Accurate P&L Data

<Tip>
  The quality of your P&L report depends entirely on the data you put in. Mark every item as Sold with its real sale price, log every expense as it happens, and track your mileage on every sourcing trip. The more complete your data, the more accurate your tax reserve estimate and the more useful your monthly breakdown will be.
</Tip>
