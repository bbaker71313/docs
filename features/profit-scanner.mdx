The Profit Scanner is the fastest way to know if something is worth buying while you're still standing in the store. Point your camera at an item, tap scan, and in seconds you get a clear HOT, LIST, or SKIP decision backed by real eBay sold comps and profit math that accounts for every fee. No mental math, no manual searching — just a confident answer before someone else grabs the item.

---

## How to scan an item

You can scan two ways: take a photo of the item or type a short description. Both routes run the same AI analysis and return the same results.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Photo Scan">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Scout tab">
        Tap the **Scout** tab at the bottom of the screen. This is your home base for all scanning.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Take a photo">
        Tap the camera icon and point your phone at the item. Try to capture brand logos, model numbers, labels, and any visible text — the more detail in the shot, the more accurate the identification.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap Scan">
        Hit **Scan** and let the AI do the work. Results typically appear in under 10 seconds.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Read your decision">
        The result screen shows your HOT / LIST / SKIP verdict, the profit estimate, and key market data. Scroll down to see the full breakdown.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Text Scan">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Scout tab">
        Tap the **Scout** tab at the bottom of the screen.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Type the item details">
        Tap the text field and enter the item name, brand, model, or any details you can read from the label. The more specific you are, the better — "Minolta X-700 35mm SLR camera" beats "old camera" every time.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap Scan">
        Hit **Scan**. The AI uses your description to look up eBay sold comps and calculate your profit.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Read your decision">
        Review the HOT / LIST / SKIP verdict and profit breakdown on the results screen.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Understanding your result: HOT, LIST, and SKIP

Every scan returns one of three verdicts. Here's what each one means:

<Note>
  The decision is based on your personal profit targets set in **Settings** — your minimum profit, target ROI, and max days to sell. Adjust those numbers to make the decisions fit how you source.

  - **HOT** — Buy immediately. The item meets your target ROI **and** the estimated profit is at least 1.5× your minimum profit threshold **and** it typically sells in under half your maximum days limit. This is a fast mover with strong upside.
  - **LIST** — Worth buying. The item clears your minimum profit, sells within your max days, and has a healthy sell-through rate. Good buy, not a slam dunk.
  - **SKIP** — Pass on it. The projected profit falls below your minimum, it takes too long to sell, or the sell-through rate is too low. Not worth the capital or the shelf space.
</Note>

### Decision style modes

Your verdict can be tightened or loosened by changing your **Style** in Settings:

- **Conservative** — Stricter thresholds. Only flags HOT or LIST when the numbers are clearly there. Good if you want to minimize bad buys.
- **Balanced** — The default. Thresholds match exactly what you set in Settings.
- **Aggressive** — Looser thresholds. More items come back as HOT or LIST. Good if you source in volume and can tolerate more misses.

## What the profit math actually means

Below the verdict you'll see a full profit breakdown. Here's what each number represents in plain English:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Avg Sold Price" icon="tag" href="/settings/fee-configuration">
    The median price this item has actually sold for on eBay recently — not what sellers are asking, not retail price. Real sold comps.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Price Range" icon="chart-bar" href="/settings/fee-configuration">
    The low-to-high spread of recent sales (roughly the 20th–80th percentile). Tells you how consistent the market is and what to realistically expect.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sell-Through Rate" icon="percent" href="/settings/decision-logic">
    The percentage of listings for this item that actually result in a sale. A 70% sell-through means 7 out of 10 listings sell. Below ~30% means slow-moving stock.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Avg Days to Sell" icon="calendar" href="/settings/decision-logic">
    How long it typically takes to go from listed to sold. Faster turnover means less capital tied up.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Demand Level" icon="signal" href="/settings/decision-logic">
    A quick read on overall demand: LOW, MEDIUM, HIGH, or VERY HIGH — based on sell-through rate and listing velocity.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Estimated Profit" icon="dollar-sign" href="/settings/fee-configuration">
    Your take-home after everything. Calculated as: **avg sold price − your cost − eBay fee − packaging − shipping.**
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

**The profit formula in plain English:**

> **Profit = Avg Sold Price − What You Pay − eBay Fee (a % of the sale price) − Packaging Cost − Shipping Cost (if you offer free shipping)**

All of those inputs come from your personal Settings. The eBay fee is applied as a percentage of the average sold price — not a flat dollar amount. Packaging and shipping costs are what you told the app you typically spend. If you charge buyers for shipping, shipping cost is not deducted. Change any of those in Settings and every scan recalculates automatically.

## Buying an item: tap BUY to add it to your inventory

When a scan comes back HOT or LIST and you decide to grab it, tap the **BUY** button on the results screen. This opens a quick confirmation screen where you can:

- Confirm or adjust the item name
- Enter what you're paying for it (your cost)
- Review the pre-filled details pulled from the scan

Hit **Confirm** and the item lands in your Inventory with everything already filled in — item name, estimated price, category, and profit projection. No retyping anything.

<Tip>
  Edit the cost field before confirming. The profit estimate assumes a typical thrift store price, but you know what you're actually paying.
</Tip>

## Tips for better scans

Getting a clean, detailed photo makes a real difference in identification accuracy.

<Tip>
  **Get the label or model number in frame.** If there's a brand name, a model plate, or a product sticker visible, make sure your photo includes it. The AI uses that text to identify the exact item — not just a generic category.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Shoot in good light.** Move the item near a window or under a bright store light before photographing. Dark or blurry photos lead to lower-confidence identifications.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Add a description even with a photo.** You can enter text in the description field alongside a photo. Use this to add details you can see but the camera might miss — like a size, color, or model number you read on the box.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Hold your phone steady and close.** Fill the frame with the item. You don't need a perfectly styled product photo — just clear enough to read the details.
</Tip>
