Profit Compass is your AI business advisor. Once a day it analyzes your actual inventory data alongside current eBay market knowledge and returns a complete business brief: a health score, your best-performing categories, specific actions for stale items, a list of items worth hunting this week, category-level market signals, and a few sentences of direct, numbers-based advice. Open the **Profit Compass** tab and read what your business is telling you.

<Note>
  Profit Compass is available on the **Stack plan and above**. It is not included on the Scout or Hustle plans. Upgrade from the Subscription tab in Profit Hub to unlock it.
</Note>

---

## Your Business Score

At the top of every Profit Compass report is your **Business Score** — a number from 0 to 100 that reflects the overall health of your reselling operation based on your inventory, sales velocity, profit margins, and stale item ratio.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Strong (75–100)" icon="circle-check">
    Your inventory is moving, margins are healthy, and you have few stale items. Keep doing what you're doing and look for ways to scale your best categories.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Growing (55–74)" icon="arrow-trend-up">
    Solid fundamentals with room to improve. You likely have some stale inventory or a category that's underperforming. Small adjustments can push you into Strong territory.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Steady (35–54)" icon="minus">
    Your business is stable but not gaining ground. Stale inventory, low-margin categories, or slow sourcing activity are holding your score down. The Stale Item Actions and Hunt List sections will tell you where to focus.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Needs Attention (0–34)" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    There are meaningful issues to address — significant stale inventory, thin or negative margins, or very limited recent sales. Work through the specific actions in your report before your next sourcing trip.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Reading Your Profit Compass Report

Each report is organized into six sections. Work through them top to bottom to get the full picture.

### Business Score

The score summary beneath the number explains the "why" in one sentence using your actual data — for example, "You have 14 active listings but 6 have been sitting for over 45 days." Use it as the headline for the rest of the report.

### Top Categories

This section lists your best-performing inventory categories ranked by total profit generated. Each entry shows:

- **Category name** — the type of item (Electronics, Clothing, Collectibles, etc.)
- **Profit generated** — the total net profit from sold items in that category
- **Insight** — one sentence specific to your data, such as "Electronics account for 68% of your profit on only 4 sales"

Use Top Categories to confirm where to focus your sourcing energy. If one category is pulling far ahead of the others, look for more of those items on your next trip.

### Stale Item Actions

Stale items are listings that have been sitting too long without selling. Profit Compass identifies each one by name and SKU and gives you a specific recommended action — no vague advice, just a direct call to action.

<Accordion title="Relist">
  The item is priced right for the market but the original listing has lost visibility. Pull it down and create a fresh listing to get back in front of buyers.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Drop price 10%">
  The item is priced above where buyers are willing to pay right now. A 10% reduction is usually enough to trigger a sale without killing your margin.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Bundle">
  The item is hard to sell on its own but has a better chance paired with something similar. Look in your inventory for a natural complement and list them together.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Donate">
  The item has been listed too long and market comps suggest it won't sell at any profitable price. Cutting it frees up your mental bandwidth and clears space for better inventory.
</Accordion>

### Hunt List

The Hunt List is a set of items the AI recommends you actively look for on your next sourcing trip, based on what's selling on eBay right now. Each entry includes:

- **Item type** — what to look for, described specifically (not just "Electronics")
- **Why** — one sentence explaining why this item is worth hunting this week
- **Priority** — HIGH or MED, so you know what to prioritize if you're short on time

<Tip>
  Review the Hunt List the night before a sourcing trip. Take a screenshot or leave Profit Compass open so you can glance at it while you're walking the floor. A HIGH-priority item on your Hunt List is worth crossing the store for.
</Tip>

### Market Trends

This section shows category-level signals based on current eBay buying patterns. Each trend includes an arrow, a category name, and a one-sentence explanation of what the signal means for thrift resellers right now.

- **📈 Rising** — buyer demand in this category is increasing. Prioritize these categories when sourcing and be willing to pay a little more at the thrift store.
- **📉 Falling** — demand is softening. Don't avoid these categories entirely, but be more selective and make sure your margin is wide enough to absorb a slower sale.

Use Market Trends alongside your Top Categories data. A category where you're already profitable *and* the trend is 📈 is a strong signal to double down.

### Advisor Message

The report closes with 3–4 sentences of direct, specific advice written using your actual numbers. This isn't generic reseller tips — it references your inventory, your margins, and your recent sales to tell you exactly what to do differently this week.

Read this section every time you open Profit Compass. It's the most actionable part of the report.

## Trending Keywords

Below your business brief, the Trending Keywords section shows the **top 10 eBay search terms right now**, ranked by search volume. Each keyword shows whether it's trending up, stable, or down.

Use trending keywords to:

- **Write stronger listing titles** — work the exact phrase buyers are searching into your title
- **Spot emerging demand** — a rising keyword you haven't been sourcing for is a hint to watch for those items
- **Validate your Hunt List** — if a Trending Keyword matches an item on your Hunt List, that's a strong confirmation

Trending Keywords refresh every 6 hours, so the data is current to within the same day.

## How Often the Report Refreshes

Profit Compass generates a new business brief **once per day**. When you open Profit Compass:

- If your report is less than 24 hours old, you see the current version instantly — no wait
- If your report is older than 24 hours, the AI brief runs automatically and the new report loads within a few seconds

You always see the freshest analysis available without having to trigger it manually.

## Getting the Most Out of Profit Compass

The quality of your Profit Compass report is directly tied to the quality of your inventory data. Follow these habits to make sure the AI has what it needs to give you useful advice.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Keep your inventory current">
    Add items to inventory when you buy them, not in batches later. The more complete your inventory, the more accurately Profit Compass can assess your stale item ratio, category mix, and sourcing pace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mark sold items with actual sale prices">
    When you sell something, mark it Sold in inventory and enter the real sale price. Profit Compass uses these figures to calculate your category profits and Business Score. Blank or placeholder prices make your score less accurate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log expenses in Profit Hub">
    Profit Compass factors in your expense data when evaluating your overall business health. Unlogged expenses make your net profit look higher than it is, which inflates your score.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Act on the Stale Item Actions">
    Don't just read the stale recommendations — work through them. Clearing dead inventory improves your Business Score, frees up capital, and signals healthier sales velocity in the next report.
  </Step>
</Steps>
