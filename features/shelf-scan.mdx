Shelf Scan lets you photograph an entire shelf at once and get every visible item ranked by profit potential in a single pass. Instead of scanning items one by one, you take one wide shot and the AI identifies everything it can see, runs the profit math on each item, and groups your results into HOT picks (buy immediately), LIST items (worth grabbing), and SKIP items (leave behind). At an estate sale, flea market, or thrift store — anywhere you're facing 20, 30, or 50 items at once — Shelf Scan cuts triage time from 30+ minutes to under a minute.

---

## How to use Shelf Scan

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Scout tab and switch to Shelf mode">
    Tap the **Scout** tab at the bottom of the screen. At the top of the scanner, switch from **Single** to **Shelf** mode.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Photograph the shelf">
    Step back far enough to get the whole shelf in frame. Hold your phone level, keep it steady, and make sure labels, logos, and text are visible. Good lighting makes a big difference — more on that below.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Scan This Shelf">
    Tap **Scan This Shelf**. The AI examines every item it can see, looks up eBay sold comps for each one, and calculates estimated profit — usually within 15–20 seconds depending on how many items are in the shot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your ranked results">
    Results appear in three groups: **HOT**, **LIST**, and **SKIP**. HOT items appear first so you see your best opportunities immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Buy what you want">
    Tap **BUY** on any item to add it to your Inventory, or keep scrolling to review everything before you decide.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding the results

Shelf Scan organizes every identified item into three groups, sorted by profit potential from highest to lowest within each group.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="🔥 HOT" icon="fire">
    Strong ROI, fast-moving, profit well above your minimum threshold. These are your priority grabs — don't leave without them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="✅ LIST" icon="check">
    Clears your minimum profit and sells within your max days. Solid buys worth picking up if you have room.
  </Card>
  <Card title="⛔ SKIP" icon="x">
    Below your profit floor, too slow to sell, or demand too low. Confirmed: leave it.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Each item in the results shows:

- **Item name** — As specific as the AI can identify: brand, model, and type
- **Avg Sold Price** — Actual recent eBay sold comps, not asking prices
- **Estimated Profit** — Your take-home after eBay fees, packaging, and shipping
- **Demand Level** — LOW, MEDIUM, HIGH, or VERY HIGH based on sell-through data

<Note>
  The AI only includes items it can identify with at least moderate confidence. If an item is out of frame, partially hidden, or too blurry to read, it won't appear in your results. This is by design — a missing item is better than a wrong price.
</Note>

## Buying items from a shelf scan

Tap **BUY** next to any item in your results to add it to Inventory. A quick confirmation screen opens where you can:

- Review the pre-filled item name and details
- Enter what you're paying (your actual cost)
- Confirm the purchase

Hit **Confirm** and it's in your Inventory — name, estimated sell price, category, and profit projection already filled in.

<Tip>
  You can buy multiple items from the same shelf scan. Tap BUY on your HOT items first, then work your way through the LIST group as time allows.
</Tip>

## Photo tips for the best results

The quality of your shelf photo directly affects how many items get identified and how accurate the pricing is. A few small habits make a big difference.

<Tip>
  **Get as close as you can while keeping the whole shelf in frame.** The AI needs to read logos, brand names, and model text. Too far away and labels become unreadable. A sweet spot is usually 3–5 feet back from the shelf.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Hold your phone level and parallel to the shelf.** Shooting at an angle introduces distortion that makes labels harder to read. Treat it like you're scanning a document — flat and straight.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Find good light before you shoot.** Move to a spot where aisle lights illuminate the shelf face directly. Avoid shooting into bright overhead lights — the glare washes out labels and creates shadows on items below. If the lighting is dim, try enabling your phone's flash.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **One shelf section at a time.** A tightly packed 8-foot run is better split into two or three shots than one wide image where everything blurs together. Overlap slightly between shots so you don't miss anything in the middle.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Reflective surfaces — glass shelves, mirrors, framed art, polished electronics — can cause glare that obscures labels. Shift your angle slightly to reduce reflections before shooting.
</Warning>

## When to use Shelf Scan vs. single item scan

Both tools pull from the same AI and the same eBay sold comps — the difference is speed versus depth.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Use Shelf Scan when…" icon="shelves">
    - You're walking an estate sale or flea market and need to triage fast
    - You're at a thrift store facing a full shelf of mixed items
    - You want to make sure you don't walk past something valuable
    - You have limited time and a lot of ground to cover
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use Single Item Scan when…" icon="magnifying-glass">
    - You've already spotted something interesting and want the full breakdown
    - You need search keywords, listing tips, or risk flags
    - The item is in a case or not on an open shelf
    - You want to add a photo directly to the item's inventory record
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

A common workflow: Shelf Scan the whole section first to find your HOT and LIST picks, then use Single Item Scan on the top contenders to dig deeper before you buy.
