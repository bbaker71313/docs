ScanForProfit runs entirely in your browser — no app download, no installation. All you need is your phone (or any device with a camera) and a few minutes. By the end of this guide you'll have a verified account, your profit settings dialed in, and your first scan result on screen.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the app">
    Go to **[scanforprofit.com/app.html](https://scanforprofit.com/app.html)** in your browser. You can bookmark it or add it to your home screen for quick access from the field.

    <Tip>
      On iPhone, tap the share icon in Safari and choose **Add to Home Screen**. On Android, tap the three-dot menu in Chrome and choose **Add to Home Screen**. ScanForProfit will appear and launch like a native app.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create your account">
    Tap **Sign Up** and enter your email address and a password of your choice. Tap **Create Account** to submit.

    Your free **Scout** plan activates immediately — no credit card required. Scout includes 25 AI scans per month and up to 10 inventory items.

    <Note>
      ScanForProfit uses email and password sign-in. There are no magic links or third-party login options. Choose a password you'll remember or save it in your password manager.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify your email">
    Check your inbox for a verification email from ScanForProfit. Open it and click the verification link to confirm your address. You must verify your email before you can log in.

    <Tip>
      If the email doesn't arrive within a couple of minutes, check your spam or junk folder. The verification link expires after a period of time, so verify soon after signing up. If the link has expired, return to the app and request a new one.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Log in">
    Return to **[scanforprofit.com/app.html](https://scanforprofit.com/app.html)**, enter your email and password, and tap **Log In**. You'll land on the **Profit Scanner** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure your profit settings">
    Before your first scan, spend 60 seconds setting up your numbers so the profit math reflects your actual business.

    1. Tap the **gear icon** at the top of the **Profit Scanner** tab to open Settings.
    2. Set your **eBay Fee %** — this is your actual final value fee rate. If you're unsure, use **13%** as a starting point for most eBay sellers.
    3. Set your **Min Profit Target** — the minimum dollar profit you'll accept on a buy. The default starting point is **$15**.
    4. Optionally update your **Packaging Cost** (default $1.25) and estimated **Shipping Cost** for a more precise profit calculation.
    5. Tap **Save Settings**.

    <Tip>
      Your eBay fee rate depends on your seller category and account standing and can range from 8% to 18%. Check your eBay Seller Hub under **Fees** to find your exact rate for the categories you sell in most.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run your first scan">
    Tap the **Profit Scanner** tab. You have two ways to scan:

    - **Photo scan:** Tap the camera button and take a clear photo of the item, or upload one from your camera roll. For a shelf scan, step back and capture the whole shelf in one wide shot.
    - **Text scan:** Tap **Type Item Name** and describe the item (e.g., "Pyrex cinderella mixing bowl set" or "Sony WH-1000XM4 headphones").

    Tap **Scan** and wait a few seconds for the result.

    <Tip>
      Get the label, brand name, or any model numbers in the frame when you photograph an item — the more detail the AI can read, the sharper the comp results will be.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Read the result">
    Your scan result shows:

    - **Decision badge** — **HOT** (high ROI, buy immediately), **LIST** (worth buying), or **SKIP** (not worth it at typical prices)
    - **Estimated sell price** — based on recent comparable sold listings on eBay
    - **Your profit** — sell price minus your cost, eBay fees, packaging, and shipping
    - **ROI %** — return on your investment
    - **Sold comps** — tap any comp to verify the price on eBay

    The decision is based on your configured profit settings. If the item clears your min profit target and target ROI, it comes back HOT or LIST. If it doesn't, you get SKIP.

    <Note>
      Sold comps are the AI's best match for the item. Always tap through to verify a comp if you're buying something expensive. The AI is your fast first read — you're the final decision-maker.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the item to inventory">
    If you're buying the item, tap **Add to Inventory**. Enter your actual purchase cost (what you're paying at the thrift store), and tap **Save**. The item is now in your **Inventory** tab with its cost, estimated sell price, and projected profit tracked automatically.

    When you're back home and ready to list, open the item in Inventory and tap **Generate Listing** to create a complete eBay listing title and description in seconds.

    <Tip>
      You've just completed the full ScanForProfit workflow: scan → decision → inventory → listing. Repeat this at every sourcing trip and your Profit Hub will start showing you exactly what you're making — by category, by month, and overall.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
